1. Product Overview

What Ledgr is, what it does, and why it exists.

Ledgr is an AI-powered accounting platform built for small businesses, freelancers, and startups that want modern, intelligent financial management without the complexity of legacy tools.

Core Capabilities

AreaWhat Ledgr Does
TransactionsTrack all income and expenses with AI auto-categorization, receipt capture, and rule-based automation.
InvoicingCreate, send, and track invoices. Convert estimates to invoices. Accept online payments (with Stripe).
AccountingFull chart of accounts, bank reconciliation, double-entry bookkeeping structure.
ReportingProfit & Loss, Balance Sheet, Cash Flow statements with CSV export.
BudgetsSet monthly budgets per category, track actual vs. planned with variance analysis.
AI AssistantAsk natural language questions about your finances. Get insights, anomaly detection, and categorization suggestions.
Tax PrepSales tax configuration, 1099 contractor identification, exportable tax data.

Tech Stack

Ledgr is built with Next.js 16, React 19, TypeScript, Tailwind CSS, Recharts for data visualization, and React Query for data management. AI features use OpenAI's GPT-4o-mini with intelligent fallbacks.

2. Who Is Ledgr For?

Five user personas and how each uses the platform.

Small Business Owner

Goal: Understand profitability, send invoices, track expenses.

Primary flow: Dashboard → Invoices → Transactions → Reports → AI Assistant

Key features: KPI cards with trend indicators, AI insights that surface anomalies, one-click invoice creation, payment reminders for overdue invoices.

Bookkeeper / Accountant

Goal: Maintain accurate books, reconcile accounts, prepare for audits.

Primary flow: Accounts → Reconciliation → Transactions → Activity Log → Reports (export)

Key features: Chart of accounts with asset/liability/equity totals, bank reconciliation with cleared/uncleared split, full audit trail, CSV export on every page.

Freelancer / Solopreneur

Goal: Simple invoicing, expense tracking, tax prep.

Primary flow: Estimates → Invoices → Transactions (with receipts) → Budgets → Settings (tax)

Key features: Estimates that convert to invoices, receipt upload via drag-and-drop, budget tracking per category, sales tax auto-calculation.

Startup CFO / Finance Lead

Goal: Monitor burn rate, control budgets, manage team access.

Primary flow: Dashboard → Budgets → Reports (Cash Flow) → Settings (Team) → AI Assistant

Key features: Budget vs. actual variance analysis, cash flow trend charts, team role management (Admin/Accountant/Viewer), AI-powered financial Q&A.

Tax Preparer

Goal: Clean financial data, 1099 prep, compliance verification.

Primary flow: Transactions (export) → Activity Log → Reports (P&L export) → Settings (sales tax)

Key features: Full-year CSV exports, 1099 contractor identification (via /api/tax), audit trail for change verification, sales tax configuration.

3. Getting Started

Your first 5 minutes with Ledgr.

Quick Setup Checklist

  1. Open the Dashboard — Navigate to Ledgr's URL. You'll see four KPI cards (Cash Balance, Income, Expenses, Net Profit), a monthly overview chart, and AI insights.
  2. Review your Chart of Accounts — Go to Accounts in the sidebar. Ledgr comes pre-configured with standard accounts (Checking, Savings, Credit Card, A/R, A/P, Equity). Add or modify as needed.
  3. Add your first transaction — Go to Transactions → click + Add Transaction. Enter a description and amount. AI will auto-categorize it. In step 2, upload the receipt.
  4. Create your first invoice — Go to Invoices → + Create Invoice. Enter client details, add line items, and set a due date.
  5. Set your budgets — Go to Budgets → Set Budget. Choose a category (e.g., Marketing) and set the monthly limit. Ledgr will track actual spending against it.
  6. Configure sales tax — Go to Settings → Sales Tax. Enable it and set your default rate. This will auto-apply to new invoices.
  7. Ask the AI — Go to AI Assistant. Try: "How much did I spend last month?" or "What's my most expensive category?"
Install Ledgr on your phone — open the app in your mobile browser, tap "Add to Home Screen." It works as a Progressive Web App with full functionality.

5. Transactions

Track every dollar in and out of your business.

Viewing Transactions

The transactions table shows all financial activity with the following columns:

ColumnDescription
DateTransaction date (formatted as "Mar 20, 2026")
DescriptionWhat the transaction is for. A ✦ sparkle icon indicates AI auto-categorized it.
CategoryExpense or income category (e.g., Software & SaaS, Client Payments)
AmountDollar amount — green for income (+), red for expenses (-)
TypeColor-coded badge: Income Expense

Filtering & Search

Use the search bar to find transactions by description. Use the type dropdown to filter by Income, Expense, or All. Results paginate at 25 per page with full navigation controls.

Adding a Transaction

  1. Step 1 — Basics: Enter description, amount, and type (expense/income/transfer).
  2. Step 2 — Details: Set the date, add optional notes, and upload a receipt (drag-and-drop or browse). AI will auto-categorize based on the description.

CSV Export

Click the CSV button next to "+ Add Transaction" to download all visible transactions as a spreadsheet file. The export includes Date, Description, Category, Amount, and Type columns.

The AI categorization engine uses a rules-based system first (configurable in the backend), then falls back to GPT-4o-mini for unknown transactions. Confidence scores are stored for each categorization.

7. Bank Reconciliation

Match your books to your bank statement — the gold standard of accurate accounting.

How Reconciliation Works

  1. Select an account — Choose which bank account to reconcile from the dropdown.
  2. Enter statement details — Input your bank statement's ending balance and statement date.
  3. Clear transactions — Check off each transaction that appears on your bank statement. The running total updates as you clear items.
  4. Resolve the difference — The summary bar shows: Book Balance, Cleared Total, Statement Balance, and Difference. Your goal is to get the difference to $0.00.
  5. Complete — When the difference is zero, click "Complete Reconciliation." A confirmation screen appears.

Reading the Summary Bar

FieldMeaning
Book BalanceThe account balance in Ledgr's records
Cleared TotalSum of all transactions you've checked off
Statement BalanceThe ending balance on your bank statement
DifferenceStatement Balance minus Cleared Total. $0.00 = balanced Non-zero = investigate
If the difference isn't zero, look for: missing transactions (not yet entered in Ledgr), duplicate entries, or transactions with wrong amounts. The two-column layout helps you compare cleared vs. uncleared items side by side.

Estimates follow a clear status flow:

Draft → Sent → Accepted → Converted to Invoice

Or: Draft → Sent → Declined

12. Reports

Three essential financial statements, charts, and CSV export.

Report Types

Profit & Loss (P&L)

Shows total income, total expenses, and net profit for the selected period. Includes a monthly trend bar chart (income vs expenses) and a donut chart breaking down expenses by category with percentages.

Balance Sheet

A point-in-time snapshot of your financial position: Total Assets, Total Liabilities, and Total Equity. Accounts are listed within each group with individual balances.

Cash Flow

Shows total inflows, outflows, opening balance, and closing balance. A line chart plots inflows, outflows, and net cash movement over time.

Controls

  • Export: Click "Export" to download the active report as CSV
  • Tab switching: Click between P&L, Balance Sheet, and Cash Flow

13. Activity Log & Audit Trail

A chronological record of every change in your account.

What Gets Logged

The Activity Log automatically tracks:

  • Transactions: Every transaction created, with amount and category
  • Invoices: Invoice creation, status changes (sent, paid, overdue)
  • Accounts: New accounts added to the chart of accounts

Features

  • Filter tabs: All, Transactions, Invoices, Accounts
  • Search: Find specific entries by description
  • Date grouping: Entries grouped by "Today," "Yesterday," or specific dates
  • Color coding: Violet for transactions, blue for invoices, green for accounts
The audit trail is essential for tax compliance and financial audits. It provides an immutable record of when changes were made. Always review the Activity Log before closing a financial period.

19. Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common questions.

Is my financial data secure?

Ledgr uses HTTPS encryption for all data in transit. When deployed on Vercel, data is served from a global edge network with enterprise-grade security. Database connections use SSL. For production use, configure authentication and role-based access in Settings.

Can I import data from QuickBooks or other tools?

Yes — export your transactions as CSV from your current tool, then import via Settings → Connected Accounts → CSV Import. The AI will auto-categorize imported transactions.

How does AI categorization work?

Ledgr uses a two-pass system: first, it checks your custom rules (configurable patterns like "Starbucks → Meals"). If no rule matches, it sends the transaction description to GPT-4o-mini for intelligent categorization with a confidence score.

What if the AI categorizes something wrong?

Edit the transaction to change its category. Over time, you can add rules to handle recurring vendors automatically. The sparkle ✦ icon marks AI-categorized transactions so you can review them.

Can multiple people use Ledgr at the same time?

Yes — Ledgr supports multiple team members with role-based access (Admin, Accountant, Viewer). Configure team members in Settings. Note: full authentication requires additional setup.

How do I get my bank transactions into Ledgr automatically?

Set up the Plaid integration by adding your PLAID_CLIENT_ID and PLAID_SECRET environment variables. Once configured, you can link bank accounts and sync transactions directly. Without Plaid, use CSV import.

Can clients pay invoices online?

Yes — with the Stripe integration active (STRIPE_SECRET_KEY), invoices get a shareable payment link. Clients see a branded payment page at /pay/[invoiceId] where they can pay via credit card. Stripe handles PCI compliance.

How do I prepare for tax season?

  1. Export all transactions as CSV from the Transactions page
  2. Export P&L report from Reports → Export
  3. Review the 1099 contractor data at /api/tax?year=2025
  4. Verify sales tax totals in Settings → Sales Tax
  5. Review the Activity Log for any discrepancies

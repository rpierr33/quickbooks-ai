### Objection Handling

**"QuickBooks integrates with everything."**
Ledgr integrates with Stripe (payments), Gusto (payroll), Shopify (e-commerce), and all major banks via Plaid. For the majority of small businesses, these cover 100% of their integration needs. If there's a specific tool you depend on, ask us — our integration roadmap is public and we prioritize based on user requests.

**"I've used QuickBooks for 10 years. My accountant knows it."**
Your accountant can be given direct access to your Ledgr account. We export standard financial reports in formats every accountant already uses. Many CPAs actively prefer Ledgr because the data is cleaner and the AI handles the categorization work they'd otherwise have to correct. We also have a growing network of Ledgr-familiar accountants.

**"Migration sounds risky."**
We have a one-click QuickBooks import. Your full transaction history, chart of accounts, and client list move in under 20 minutes. Most businesses run Ledgr in parallel with QuickBooks for their first month-end close, then cancel QuickBooks once they're confident. There is no forced timeline.

**"QuickBooks is what my bank / lender requires."**
Ledgr produces GAAP-compliant financial statements in standard formats. If your bank or lender has specific requirements, contact us and we'll verify compatibility. In every case we've tested, Ledgr output meets standard lending requirements.

**"QuickBooks has a mobile app."**
Ledgr has a full mobile app. Receipt scanning, invoice creation, and financial snapshot are all accessible from your phone.

---

### Switching Guide Summary

1. Export from QuickBooks: Transaction List CSV + Chart of Accounts CSV (10 min)
2. Import to Ledgr: Settings → Import → QuickBooks (10 min processing)
3. Review and approve AI recategorization (20 min first time)
4. Run your first month in parallel (both systems)
5. Cancel QuickBooks before next billing cycle

Total active time: ~1 hour. Timeline: 30 days to be confident.

### The One-Line Positioning

"Wave is free accounting. Ledgr is AI-powered accounting starting at $29.99/mo. If you're spending time categorizing transactions or building reports manually, Ledgr pays for itself on day 30."

---

---

### The Cost vs. Time Question

Wave is free. Ledgr starts at $29.99/month. The meaningful comparison is time cost vs. money cost.

Wave's free tier requires manual categorization of every transaction. For a business with 100 transactions/month at 30 seconds each, that's 50 minutes of manual work per month. Per year: 10 hours.

Ledgr's Starter tier uses AI to pre-categorize. Same 100 transactions → 5 minutes of review per month. Per year: 1 hour.

At a modest $50/hour value of your time, Ledgr's $29.99/month pays for itself 3x over on time savings alone.

---

### Ideal Customer Profile: Switch to Ledgr from Wave

- Business owners who value their time more than the effort of manual entry
- Growing businesses that need better reporting and forecasting
- Any Wave user frustrated by support response times
- Businesses that want AI features without adding another tool

---

### Objection Handling

**"Wave is free and I'm comfortable with it."**
Comfort is worth something. If Wave does everything you need and you don't mind manual categorization, stay with Wave. The switch to Ledgr is most compelling for businesses at the point where bookkeeping is becoming a meaningful time cost — usually 50+ transactions per month. At that volume, Ledgr's $29.99/month saves you more time than it costs.

**"Wave is backed by H&R Block."**
H&R Block acquired Wave in 2019. This provides financial stability. It has also meant Wave's product development has slowed — no major AI features have shipped in 4 years. Ledgr is built AI-first for the current moment.

**"Wave handles my payroll."**
Ledgr's Payroll Add-on is available for Business and Enterprise subscribers at $20/mo + $5/employee. For Starter and Professional subscribers, Ledgr integrates with Gusto and ADP. We'll have native payroll at all tiers in Q3 2026.

- Mature product with extensive feature set at higher tiers

### Where Ledgr Wins

| Free tier revenue cap | $50K/yr (Standard at $20+ after) | No free tier — 14-day full trial, then $29.99/mo |
| AI quality | Zoho AI (Zia) — general-purpose | GPT-4o — purpose-built for accounting |
| AI chat (accounting-specific) | No | Yes |
| Ecosystem requirement | Works best with Zoho suite | Standalone; open integrations |

| Time-to-value | Complex (many modules) | Fast — connect bank, AI does rest |
| Starting price (sustainable) | $20/mo (Standard — after free cap hit) | $29.99/mo (Starter — no cap) |

---

### The AI Comparison (Critical Differentiator)

Zoho Books uses Zoho AI (branded "Zia"). Zia is a general-purpose AI assistant that covers CRM, project management, email, and accounting. It is not built specifically for accounting.

Ledgr uses GPT-4o, fine-tuned and prompted for accounting tasks specifically. The difference in practice:

- Zoho Zia can answer basic questions about your data
- Ledgr AI can answer "What's my effective tax rate on revenue vs. cash basis?" and give you the GAAP breakdown

For a business that lives in the Zoho ecosystem, Zoho Books is sensible. For a business that wants the best AI accounting without ecosystem lock-in, Ledgr is the cleaner choice.

---

### Ideal Customer Profile: Switch to Ledgr from Zoho Books

- Businesses that signed up for Zoho Books free tier and are hitting the $50K revenue cap
- Businesses using Zoho Books standalone (not Zoho CRM/Projects) who want better AI
- US businesses that want US-first tax support and 1099 tracking
- Companies frustrated with Zoho's support quality or product complexity

---

### Objection Handling

**"Zoho Books has a free tier."**
Zoho Books free is limited to businesses with under $50K annual revenue — that's $4,166/month. Most growing businesses have already exceeded that. Ledgr's 14-day full-access trial lets you evaluate the full product before committing. After the trial, Starter at $29.99/month has no revenue cap.

**"I use other Zoho products."**
If you're heavily invested in Zoho CRM and Zoho Projects, the Zoho Books integration is a genuine advantage. The question is whether that integration justifies using a less capable accounting tool. Many businesses use Ledgr for accounting and connect to their Zoho CRM via Zapier or API.

**"Zoho is more established internationally."**
Yes — Zoho is headquartered in India and has strong presence in South Asian, European, and Middle Eastern markets. If your business operates in those markets, Zoho's international tax and currency features may be more relevant. Ledgr is US-primary, with international expansion on the 18-month roadmap.

## Universal Objection Responses

### "You're a new company. How do I know you'll be around in 5 years?"

Three things worth knowing:

1. We're funded and the business is growing. Specific numbers are not public, but we are not a side project.
2. Your data is exportable at any time in standard formats (CSV, PDF). If Ledgr ever shut down, you would not lose your books.
3. The accounting software market has survived new entrants for 30 years. The incumbents (QuickBooks, FreshBooks) were once new companies too.

### "I don't want to spend time learning new software."

Most Ledgr users are productive within 20 minutes of their first bank connection. The AI handles the categorization that usually requires the most learning. There is no chart of accounts setup wizard on day one — you can let AI categorize and refine over time.

We offer free onboarding calls for all new users. If you want a guided walkthrough, book one.

### "The pricing seems too good. What's the real catch?"

There isn't one. We charge for real capacity: users, invoice volume, and advanced features like inventory and API access. We don't create artificial limits to force upgrades — we charge for features that actually cost more to deliver. The 14-day full-access trial exists because we're confident the product earns the subscription.

Paid plans start at $29.99/mo for solo users. It's honest pricing for a product that delivers real value.

### "Can you integrate with [specific tool]?"

Current integrations: Stripe, Gusto, ADP, Shopify, PayPal, Square, all major US banks via Plaid, CSV import/export.

Roadmap integrations (2026): QuickBooks migration tool (one-click), Xero migration tool, HubSpot, Salesforce.

For anything else: submit a request at ledgr.com/integrations. We build the most-requested integrations first.

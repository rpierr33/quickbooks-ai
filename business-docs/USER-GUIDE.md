# Ledgr User Guide

### Complete Guide to AI-Powered Accounting for Your Business

---

## Welcome to Ledgr

Ledgr is bookkeeping software built for people who run businesses, not accountants. You do not need to know what a "debit" is or understand the difference between a balance sheet and a P&L to use Ledgr effectively. The AI handles the technical accounting so you can focus on running your business.

This guide walks through every part of Ledgr from setting up your account to generating reports at tax time. Refer to the specific section you need — you do not need to read this front to back.

**Bold text** = the name of a button, field, or section in the app.

## 1. Getting Started

### 1.1 Creating Your Account

1. Go to the Ledgr website and click **Get Started Free**.
2. Enter your email address and create a password. Your password must be at least 8 characters.
3. Click **Create Account**.
4. You will be automatically signed in and taken to the **Onboarding** screen.

**Demo account:** If you want to explore Ledgr before creating an account, use the demo login:
- Email: `demo@ledgr.com`
- Password: `demo`

The demo account includes pre-loaded data so you can see how the app looks with real transactions, invoices, and reports.

### 1.2 First-Time Setup (Onboarding)

The onboarding screen collects the basics Ledgr needs to set up your account properly.

**Step 1 — Company Details**
- **Company Name:** The legal name of your business, or your full name if you are a sole proprietor.
- **Fiscal Year Start:** Most businesses use January. If your business's tax year starts in a different month (common for businesses with seasonal cycles), select that month here.

**Step 2 — Business Type**
Select the type that best describes your business:
- **Standard** — Most businesses: retail, restaurants, trades
- **Service** — Freelancers, consultants, agencies
- **Retail** — Businesses that buy and resell physical goods
- **Nonprofit** — Tax-exempt organizations

This selection sets up your Chart of Accounts with the right categories for your industry. You can change this later in **Settings**.

**Step 3 — Confirm**
Review your selections and click **Finish Setup**. You will be taken to your **Dashboard**.

### 1.3 Logging In

1. Go to the Ledgr website and click **Log In**.
2. Enter your email and password.
3. Click **Sign In**.

If you have forgotten your password, click **Forgot password?** and follow the instructions sent to your email.

### 1.4 Navigation

The sidebar on the left side of the screen is your main navigation. It is organized into sections:

**Money In**
- Invoices
- Estimates
- Clients

**Money Out**
- Transactions
- Receipts

**Accounting**
- Accounts
- Journal Entries
- Reconciliation
- Budgets
- Recurring

**Reports**
- All 7 financial reports

**AI**
- Chat
- Insights

**Settings**
- Company, team, preferences

On mobile, the sidebar collapses to a hamburger menu at the top of the screen. Tap the icon to open it.

## 2. Dashboard

The **Dashboard** is your financial overview. It shows you what is happening in your business right now, without making you run a report.

### 2.1 What You See on the Dashboard

**Financial Stats (top row)**
Four key numbers at a glance:
- **Total Revenue** — All income from invoices and transactions marked as income
- **Total Expenses** — All spending this period
- **Net Income** — Revenue minus expenses (your profit)
- **Outstanding Invoices** — The total amount you are owed by clients

Each stat shows the current month's value and a comparison to the previous month (for example, "+12% vs last month").

**Revenue vs. Expenses Chart**
A bar chart showing your income and spending side by side, month by month, for the last 12 months. This answers the question: "Is my revenue trending up? Are my expenses staying under control?" at a glance.

**Cash Flow Forecast**
An AI-generated projection of your cash position for the next 30, 60, and 90 days based on your transaction history. This helps you see potential cash crunches before they happen. Click on the forecast section to see a more detailed breakdown.

**Recent Transactions**
The last 5 transactions in your account. Click any transaction to view and edit it. Click **View all** to go to the Transactions page.

**Outstanding Invoices**
A list of your unpaid invoices sorted by due date. Invoices that are overdue are highlighted in red. Click any invoice to view its full details or mark it as paid.

**AI Insights**
A brief, plain-English summary of notable patterns in your finances this month. Examples: "You spent 22% more on supplies this month compared to your average." or "You have 3 invoices totaling $4,200 that are more than 30 days overdue."

### 2.2 Changing the Date Range

By default, the Dashboard shows the current month. To see a different period:
1. Click the date selector in the top right corner of the dashboard.
2. Select a preset (This Week, This Month, This Quarter, This Year) or enter custom dates.
3. The stats and chart will update immediately.

### 2.3 Quick Actions

The **Quick Actions** button (top right, or the **+** icon on mobile) lets you create common items without navigating to a specific section:
- **New Invoice**
- **New Transaction**
- **New Expense**
- **Scan Receipt**

## 3. Transactions

Transactions are the individual income and expense records in your account. Every time money moves — a payment from a client, a purchase from a vendor, a subscription charge — it lives here as a transaction.

### 3.1 The Transactions List

Go to **Transactions** in the sidebar.

You will see a table with columns for:
- **Date**
- **Description** — What the transaction is for
- **Category** — The accounting category (e.g., "Office Supplies," "Client Payment")
- **Account** — Which bank account or credit card this transaction came from
- **Amount** — Positive = income, Negative (red) = expense

### 3.2 Adding a Transaction Manually

1. Click **+ Add Transaction**.
2. Fill in the fields:
   - **Date** — When the transaction happened
   - **Description** — A brief note (e.g., "Adobe Creative Cloud subscription")
   - **Amount** — Enter the dollar amount. Do NOT include a minus sign for expenses — use the **Type** field instead
   - **Type** — Select **Expense** or **Income**
   - **Category** — Select from the dropdown, or let Ledgr's AI suggest one based on the description
   - **Account** — Which bank account or credit card this belongs to
   - **Notes** (optional) — Any additional context
3. Click **Save Transaction**.

**Tip:** If you type a description, Ledgr's AI will often pre-fill the **Category** field automatically. Review the suggestion and change it if the AI got it wrong.

### 3.3 Editing a Transaction

1. Click on any transaction in the list.
2. The transaction detail panel opens on the right.
3. Click **Edit**.
4. Make your changes.
5. Click **Save**.

### 3.4 Deleting a Transaction

1. Click on the transaction.
2. Click the **...** menu (three dots) in the top right corner of the detail panel.
3. Select **Delete Transaction**.
4. Confirm the deletion.

**Note:** Deleting a transaction is permanent. This will affect your reports and account balances.

### 3.5 Searching and Filtering

**Search:** Type in the search bar at the top of the Transactions page. Ledgr searches the description, category, and notes fields.

**Filters (click the Filter icon):**
- **Date range** — Narrow to a specific period
- **Type** — Show only Income or only Expenses
- **Category** — Show only transactions in a specific category
- **Account** — Show only transactions from one bank account
- **Amount range** — Show only transactions above or below a dollar amount

Multiple filters can be applied at once.

### 3.6 Importing Transactions from a CSV File

If you have transactions in a spreadsheet (exported from your bank, for example), you can import them in bulk:

1. Click **Import CSV**.
2. Download the **CSV template** (link is on the import page) to see the required column format.
3. Format your spreadsheet to match the template: Date, Description, Amount, Type (Income/Expense), Category (optional).
4. Save your spreadsheet as a .csv file.
5. Click **Upload CSV** and select your file.
6. Review the preview — Ledgr will show you a sample of what will be imported.
7. Click **Confirm Import**.

Imported transactions go through the same AI categorization as manually entered ones. Review any transactions where the category says "Uncategorized" after import.

**Supported CSV formats:**
- Ledgr template (recommended)
- Generic bank export format (Ledgr will attempt to map columns automatically)

**Limits:** Up to 1,000 transactions per import. For larger imports, split into multiple files.

### 3.7 Categories

Categories tell Ledgr what each transaction is for. They map to your Chart of Accounts and appear in your financial reports.

**Default income categories:**
- Client Payments
- Product Sales
- Service Revenue
- Consulting Income
- Other Income

**Default expense categories:**
- Advertising and Marketing
- Bank Charges
- Computer and Software
- Insurance
- Meals and Entertainment
- Office Supplies
- Professional Services
- Rent and Utilities
- Repairs and Maintenance
- Salaries and Wages
- Shipping and Delivery
- Travel
- Taxes and Licenses
- Other Expenses

You can add custom categories in **Settings > Categories**.

## 4. Invoicing

Invoices are how you bill clients for work completed. Ledgr's invoices generate a professional PDF, track payment status, and feed directly into your financial reports.

### 4.1 Creating an Invoice

1. Go to **Invoices** in the sidebar.
2. Click **+ New Invoice**.
3. Fill in the invoice details:

**Client:** Select an existing client from the dropdown, or type a new name to create a new client on the fly.

**Invoice Number:** Ledgr auto-generates an invoice number (e.g., INV-0001). You can change this to match your own numbering system.

**Invoice Date:** Defaults to today. Change if needed.

**Due Date:** Select when payment is due. Common options: Net 15, Net 30, Net 60, or a custom date.

**Line Items:** Add one or more items to the invoice.
- Click **+ Add Line Item**.
- Enter the **Description** (what you're billing for), **Quantity**, and **Unit Price**.
- The **Amount** calculates automatically.
- Add more line items as needed.

**Tax:** If you charge sales tax, enter the tax rate (e.g., 8.5). Ledgr calculates the tax amount.

**Discount:** Enter a percentage discount if applicable.

**Notes:** Add payment instructions, thank-you text, or any other message for the client.

4. Review the **Total** at the bottom.
5. Click **Save Invoice** to save as a draft, or **Save and Send** to immediately generate the PDF.

### 4.2 Invoice Statuses

| Status | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **Draft** | Created but not yet sent to client |
| **Sent** | Marked as sent (you handle delivery — Ledgr does not email the client on the Starter plan) |
| **Paid** | Payment received and recorded |
| **Overdue** | Past the due date and not paid |
| **Cancelled** | Voided, no payment expected |

### 4.3 Generating and Downloading the PDF

1. Open an invoice.
2. Click **Download PDF**.
3. Ledgr generates a professional invoice PDF with your company name, client details, line items, and totals.
4. Save the PDF and attach it to your email to the client.

**Customizing the PDF:** You can add your company logo in **Settings > Company Profile**. The logo will appear on all generated PDFs.

### 4.4 Marking an Invoice as Paid

When a client pays:
1. Open the invoice.
2. Click **Mark as Paid**.
3. Enter the **Payment Date** and **Payment Method** (Bank Transfer, Credit Card, Check, Cash, etc.).
4. Click **Confirm**.

Ledgr automatically creates a corresponding transaction in your Transactions list for the payment.

### 4.5 Tracking Outstanding Invoices

On the **Dashboard**, the **Outstanding Invoices** section shows all unpaid invoices. You can also see them by filtering the Invoices list by status **Sent** or **Overdue**.

Invoices that are overdue are highlighted in red in the Invoices list and on the Dashboard.

### 4.6 Editing an Invoice

Invoices in **Draft** status can be edited freely. Invoices in **Sent** or **Paid** status should only be edited carefully, as changes affect your financial records.

To edit: Open the invoice and click **Edit Invoice**.

To create a corrected version of a paid invoice: click **... > Duplicate** to create a copy, then void the original.

## 5. Estimates

Estimates (also called quotes) are proposals you send to potential clients before doing the work. Once approved, an estimate converts to an invoice with one click.

### 5.1 Creating an Estimate

1. Go to **Estimates** in the sidebar.
2. Click **+ New Estimate**.
3. Fill in the details — the form is identical to the invoice form (client, line items, dates), with two differences:
   - **Expiry Date** instead of Due Date — when the quote expires
   - Status starts as **Draft**, not **Sent**
4. Click **Save Estimate**.

### 5.2 Estimate Statuses

| Status | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **Draft** | Created, not yet sent |
| **Sent** | Shared with client |
| **Accepted** | Client has approved |
| **Declined** | Client passed |
| **Invoiced** | Converted to an invoice |
| **Expired** | Past the expiry date |

### 5.3 Converting an Estimate to an Invoice

When a client accepts your estimate:
1. Open the estimate.
2. Click **Convert to Invoice**.
3. Ledgr creates a new invoice with all the same line items, client, and pricing pre-filled.
4. Review the invoice and set the **Due Date**.
5. Click **Save Invoice**.

The estimate status changes to **Invoiced** and a link to the resulting invoice appears on the estimate detail page.

## 7. Chart of Accounts

The **Chart of Accounts** is a list of all the financial categories that Ledgr uses to organize your money. Think of it as the master list of "buckets" that transactions get sorted into.

You do not need to understand accounting to use Ledgr's chart of accounts — the defaults work for most businesses. This section is for those who want to customize it.

### 7.1 Account Types

Ledgr organizes accounts into five standard types:

| Type | Examples | What It Tracks |
|------|----------|---------------|
| **Assets** | Checking, Savings, Accounts Receivable | What you own |
| **Liabilities** | Credit Cards, Loans, Accounts Payable | What you owe |
| **Equity** | Owner's Equity, Retained Earnings | Your ownership stake |
| **Income** | Sales Revenue, Consulting Income | Money coming in |
| **Expenses** | Rent, Salaries, Office Supplies | Money going out |

### 7.2 Viewing Your Accounts

Go to **Accounts** in the sidebar. You will see all accounts grouped by type. Each account shows:
- Account name
- Account number (optional)
- Account type
- Current balance

### 7.3 Adding an Account

1. Click **+ New Account**.
2. Fill in:
   - **Account Name** — A descriptive name (e.g., "Business Checking," "Amazon Credit Card")
   - **Account Type** — Asset, Liability, Equity, Income, or Expense
   - **Account Number** — Optional, for internal reference
   - **Description** — Optional notes
3. Click **Save Account**.

### 7.4 Editing an Account

Click on any account and click **Edit** to update its name, number, or description.

**Note:** You cannot change an account's type after transactions have been assigned to it.

### 7.5 What the CoA Templates Set Up

When you selected your business type during onboarding, Ledgr set up a chart of accounts tailored to your industry. A **Service** business gets accounts like "Consulting Revenue" and "Contractor Expenses." A **Retail** business gets "Product Sales" and "Cost of Goods Sold."

If you want to reset to a different template:
1. Go to **Settings > Company Profile**.
2. Click **Change CoA Template**.
3. Select the new template.
4. Confirm — this will not delete existing transactions, but new categories will be added.

## 8. Financial Reports

Ledgr includes 7 financial reports. Each one can be generated for any date range and exported as a PDF or CSV.

### 8.1 Accessing Reports

Go to **Reports** in the sidebar. You will see all 7 report types listed.

For each report:
1. Click the report name.
2. Set the **Date Range** at the top.
3. The report generates automatically.
4. Click **Export PDF** or **Export CSV** to download.

### 8.2 Profit & Loss (P&L)

**What it shows:** Your revenue, expenses, and net profit for a period.

**How to read it:**
- **Revenue** section: Total income from all income accounts
- **Cost of Goods Sold** (if applicable): Direct costs to produce your product or service
- **Gross Profit:** Revenue minus Cost of Goods Sold
- **Expenses:** All operating expenses by category
- **Net Income (or Net Loss):** Gross Profit minus Expenses

**When to use it:** Monthly financial check-in, tax time, sharing with investors or lenders.

### 8.3 Balance Sheet

**What it shows:** A snapshot of your financial position at a specific date — what you own (assets), what you owe (liabilities), and what is left over (equity).

**How to read it:**
- **Assets** (top): Everything your business owns (cash, accounts receivable, equipment)
- **Liabilities** (middle): Everything your business owes (loans, credit card balances, unpaid bills)
- **Equity** (bottom): The difference — what the business is worth to you

The total of Assets should always equal Liabilities plus Equity. If it does not, there is a data entry error somewhere.

**When to use it:** Year-end reporting, loan applications, due diligence for investors.

### 8.4 Cash Flow Statement

**What it shows:** Where cash came from and where it went during a period. Different from the P&L because it tracks actual cash movements, not just invoices.

**Sections:**
- **Operating Activities:** Cash from your core business operations
- **Investing Activities:** Cash from buying or selling assets
- **Financing Activities:** Cash from loans or owner contributions

**When to use it:** Understand why your bank account balance changed. A profitable business can still have cash problems — the Cash Flow Statement shows why.

### 8.5 Tax Summary

**What it shows:** Your income and expenses organized by tax category. Designed to make it easy to fill out a Schedule C (for sole proprietors) or provide data to your accountant.

**How to use it:**
1. Set the date range to your tax year (usually January 1 – December 31).
2. The report groups all expenses into IRS-relevant categories.
3. Export as PDF and send to your accountant, or use the numbers to fill in your tax form yourself.

### 8.6 Trial Balance

**What it shows:** Every account in your chart of accounts with its total debits, total credits, and ending balance. This is an internal accounting report used to verify that your books are in balance.

**When to use it:** Primarily for accountants verifying the accuracy of your records. You may not need this report regularly.

### 8.7 Aged Receivables

**What it shows:** All your unpaid invoices grouped by how long they have been outstanding.

| Bucket | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **Current** | Not yet due |
| **1–30 days** | Up to 30 days past due |
| **31–60 days** | 31–60 days past due |
| **61–90 days** | 61–90 days past due |
| **90+ days** | More than 90 days past due |

**When to use it:** Cash flow management. Clients in the 60+ day buckets need follow-up. Clients in the 90+ day bucket may need to be sent to collections.

### 8.8 General Ledger

**What it shows:** Every journal entry and transaction, sorted by account. This is the most detailed report in Ledgr — a complete record of every financial event.

**When to use it:** Deep investigation of a specific account (e.g., "show me every transaction that hit my 'Advertising' account this year"). Your accountant will want this at year-end.

## 9. AI Features

Ledgr includes six AI-powered features that run automatically or are available on demand. All AI features require an internet connection and work best with at least 30 days of transaction history.

### 9.1 AI Transaction Categorization

**What it does:** When you add a transaction with a description, Ledgr's AI reads the description and automatically suggests (or assigns) a category from your chart of accounts.

**How it works:**
- Add a transaction with a description like "Adobe Creative Cloud"
- Ledgr sees this and assigns "Computer and Software"
- You can review and change the suggestion before saving

**Reviewing AI-categorized transactions:**
Transactions that were AI-categorized show a small robot icon (or "AI" badge) next to the category. This means a human has not reviewed it yet. To do a bulk review:
1. Go to **Transactions**.
2. Filter by **AI Categorized: Yes**.
3. Review each one. If the category is correct, click **Confirm**. If not, change it.

The AI learns from your corrections — if you regularly change "contractor payments" to "Professional Services," it will start getting that right automatically.

### 9.2 AI Chat Assistant

**What it does:** Lets you ask questions about your finances in plain English and get answers instantly.

**How to access it:** Click **AI Chat** in the sidebar or the chat icon in the bottom-right corner.

**What you can ask:**
- "How much have I made this year?"
- "What was my most expensive month for travel?"
- "Which clients owe me money right now?"
- "Am I spending more or less on marketing compared to last quarter?"
- "What's my average invoice amount?"
- "Show me all my expenses over $500 this month"

The AI has access to your actual transaction and invoice data, so the answers are based on your real numbers — not generic advice.

**Limitations:**
- The AI does not give tax or legal advice.
- For very large datasets (10,000+ transactions), some queries may be slower.
- The AI cannot take actions (create transactions, send invoices) — it can only answer questions.

### 9.3 Cash Flow Forecasting

**What it does:** Projects your cash position for the next 30, 60, and 90 days based on your income and expense patterns.

**Where to find it:** The **Dashboard** shows a summary. Click on the forecast widget for a full breakdown, or go to **AI > Forecasting**.

**How it works:**
Ledgr analyzes:
- Your average monthly income over the past 3–6 months
- Your average monthly expenses by category
- Seasonal patterns in your data
- Outstanding invoices (expected income when paid)
- Recurring transactions (predictable future expenses)

**Reading the forecast:**
- Green = projected positive cash position
- Yellow = projected to get tight (under 30 days of expenses in reserves)
- Red = projected cash shortfall

**Important:** The forecast is a projection, not a guarantee. It assumes historical patterns continue. Unexpected large expenses or lost clients will not be reflected.

### 9.4 AI Insights Panel

**What it does:** Surfaces notable patterns, anomalies, and trends in your financial data, written in plain English.

**Where to find it:** On the Dashboard as a card, or go to **AI > Insights**.

**Examples of insights Ledgr generates:**
- "Your revenue is 18% higher this month compared to the same month last year."
- "You have 3 clients who haven't paid in over 60 days, totaling $6,800."
- "Your 'Advertising' expenses spiked 120% this month — highest in 12 months."
- "You currently have enough cash to cover 2.3 months of average expenses."
- "Your largest expense category is 'Salaries,' which makes up 54% of total spending."

Insights refresh automatically when new transactions are added or once per day.

### 9.5 Category Suggestions (Real-Time)

When you type in the **Description** field while adding a transaction, Ledgr's AI suggests categories in real time (dropdown below the field). This is different from the automatic categorization above — this shows you the suggestion before you save, so you can accept or override it immediately.

### 9.6 AI Quality and Accuracy

The AI uses OpenAI's GPT-4o-mini model. It is accurate for common transaction types (major software vendors, well-known retailers, common business expenses). It may make mistakes on:
- Unusual vendors with non-obvious names
- Transactions with very short or generic descriptions (e.g., "Payment" or "Transfer")
- Industry-specific expenses with unusual terminology

Always review AI-categorized transactions before relying on them for tax filing.

## 10. Receipt Scanner

The Receipt Scanner lets you photograph a receipt and have Ledgr automatically create an expense transaction from it.

### 10.1 Scanning a Receipt

1. Click **Scan Receipt** (in the sidebar or via **Quick Actions**).
2. Upload a photo or scan of your receipt. Supported formats: JPG, PNG, PDF.
3. Ledgr's AI reads the receipt and extracts:
   - **Vendor name** (who you paid)
   - **Date** (when you paid)
   - **Total amount**
   - **Category** (suggested based on the vendor)
4. Review the extracted information in the preview pane.
5. Correct any fields where the AI made an error.
6. Click **Create Transaction** to save it.

### 10.2 Tips for Better Scan Results

- Use good lighting. Avoid shadows on the receipt.
- Make sure the entire receipt is visible in the photo — no cut-off edges.
- Flatten crumpled receipts before scanning.
- For long receipts, take one photo that captures the full receipt (including the total at the bottom).
- PDFs work as well as images — download digital receipts as PDF directly.

### 10.3 What Happens After Scanning

The transaction is created in your Transactions list just like a manually entered transaction, with the vendor name as the description and the extracted category. The receipt image is attached to the transaction so you have a digital copy for your records.

### 10.4 Scanning Multiple Receipts

Scan one receipt at a time. There is no current batch-scan feature. If you have a large stack of receipts, work through them one at a time — each scan takes about 10–15 seconds.

## 11. Bank Reconciliation

Bank reconciliation is the process of matching the transactions in Ledgr to the actual transactions on your bank statement. It confirms that your Ledgr records are accurate and catches any transactions you missed or entered incorrectly.

Most businesses reconcile once a month, after the bank statement closes.

### 11.1 How Reconciliation Works

1. Go to **Reconciliation** in the sidebar.
2. Select the **Account** you want to reconcile (e.g., your business checking account).
3. Enter the **Statement Date** — the end date on your bank statement.
4. Enter the **Ending Balance** — the closing balance on your bank statement.
5. Click **Start Reconciliation**.

Ledgr shows you two columns:
- **Ledgr Transactions** — all transactions you have entered in Ledgr for this account and period
- **Bank Transactions** (if bank feeds are connected) — transactions directly from your bank

### 11.2 Matching Transactions

Go through the Ledgr transactions list and check off each one that matches your bank statement.

- If a transaction appears on your bank statement AND in Ledgr: check it off.
- If a transaction is on your bank statement but NOT in Ledgr: add it (click **+ Add Missing Transaction**).
- If a transaction is in Ledgr but NOT on your bank statement: investigate — it may be a duplicate entry or an error.

The **Difference** counter at the top shows the gap between your Ledgr records and the bank statement. Your goal is to get this to $0.00.

### 11.3 Finishing Reconciliation

When the difference reaches $0.00:
1. Click **Finish Reconciliation**.
2. Ledgr marks all matched transactions as reconciled.
3. A reconciliation record is saved with the statement date, ending balance, and reconciled transactions.

### 11.4 Connecting Bank Feeds (Starter Plan and Above)

With a Starter plan ($29.99/mo) or above, you can connect your bank account through Plaid so transactions import automatically instead of being entered manually.

1. Go to **Settings > Connected Accounts**.
2. Click **Connect Bank Account**.
3. Follow the Plaid secure bank connection flow (you will be redirected to your bank's login).
4. Select the accounts you want to connect.
5. Click **Connect**.

Once connected, Ledgr pulls in transactions automatically every night. New imported transactions appear in your Transactions list with an **Imported** badge and need to be reviewed and confirmed.

**Security:** Ledgr never stores your bank username or password. The connection is handled entirely by Plaid, which is used by thousands of financial apps and is SOC 2 certified.

## 12. Budgets

Budgets let you set monthly spending targets for each category and track whether you are staying on track.

### 12.1 Creating a Budget

1. Go to **Budgets** in the sidebar.
2. Click **+ New Budget**.
3. Give the budget a name (e.g., "2026 Annual Budget" or "Q2 Operating Budget").
4. Set the **Period** — Monthly, Quarterly, or Annual.
5. Set a **Start Date**.
6. For each expense category, enter a budget amount.
   - You do not have to budget every category — leave categories blank if you are not tracking them.
7. Click **Save Budget**.

### 12.2 Tracking Your Budget

Once a budget is saved, go to the budget detail page to see your progress.

For each budgeted category:
- **Budgeted:** The amount you planned to spend
- **Actual:** What you have actually spent (pulled from your transactions)
- **Variance:** The difference (green = under budget, red = over budget)
- **Progress bar:** Visual indicator of budget consumption

If you are 75% through the month and 90% through your budget for a category, it is highlighted as a warning.

### 12.3 Adjusting a Budget Mid-Period

Life happens. If you need to change a budget target:
1. Open the budget.
2. Click **Edit Budget**.
3. Update the category amounts.
4. Click **Save**.

Changes take effect immediately — the variance calculation updates to reflect the new targets.

## 13. Recurring Transactions

Recurring transactions are expenses or income that happen on a fixed schedule. Setting them up in Ledgr ensures they are automatically created in your transaction list so they never get missed.

**Examples:**
- Monthly SaaS subscriptions (Slack, Adobe, Notion)
- Weekly payroll runs
- Quarterly insurance payments
- Monthly rent

### 13.1 Creating a Recurring Transaction

1. Go to **Recurring** in the sidebar.
2. Click **+ New Recurring Transaction**.
3. Fill in the fields:
   - **Description** — What the transaction is (e.g., "Slack subscription")
   - **Amount** — The recurring amount
   - **Type** — Expense or Income
   - **Category** — The category to assign
   - **Account** — Which account it comes from
   - **Frequency** — Daily, Weekly, Bi-weekly, Monthly, Quarterly, Annually
   - **Start Date** — When the first transaction should be created
   - **End Date** (optional) — Leave blank for indefinite recurring
4. Click **Save**.

### 13.2 How Recurring Transactions Work

Ledgr creates the transaction automatically on the scheduled date. The transaction appears in your Transactions list with a **Recurring** badge.

If the amount varies (e.g., utilities), edit the generated transaction after it is created.

### 13.3 Pausing or Stopping a Recurring Transaction

1. Go to **Recurring** and find the template.
2. Click on it.
3. Click **Pause** to temporarily stop it (it can be resumed later), or **Delete** to remove the template permanently.

Pausing does not delete past transactions — only future scheduled ones are stopped.

## 15. FAQ

**Q: Do I need to know accounting to use Ledgr?**
No. Ledgr is designed for business owners, not accountants. The AI handles categorization and the reports are written in plain English. If you can track income and expenses in a spreadsheet, you can use Ledgr.

**Q: Is my financial data secure?**
Yes. Your data is encrypted in transit (TLS 1.3) and at rest. Bank connections are handled by Plaid, which never shares your banking credentials with Ledgr. We do not sell your data to third parties.

**Q: Can I import my data from QuickBooks?**
Yes. Export your transactions from QuickBooks as a CSV file, then use Ledgr's **Import CSV** feature to bring them in. For invoices and clients, the import tool accepts the QuickBooks export format.

**Q: How accurate is the AI categorization?**
For common transactions (major software vendors, well-known retailers, standard business expenses), the AI is accurate over 85% of the time based on internal testing. For unusual vendors or very short descriptions ("Payment", "Transfer"), accuracy drops. Always review AI-suggested categories before filing taxes.

**Q: What happens to my data if I cancel my subscription?**
Your data is retained for 90 days after cancellation. During that period you can log in and export all your data. After 90 days, data is permanently deleted. You can reactivate your account within 90 days and all data will still be there.

**Q: Can I use Ledgr for multiple businesses?**
Each Ledgr account is for one business. To manage a second business, create a separate account with a different email address. Multi-entity management (single login, multiple orgs) is on the Enterprise roadmap for late 2026.

**Q: Does Ledgr file my taxes?**
No. Ledgr generates the reports and data you need to file taxes, but it does not file your taxes. Take the Tax Summary report to your accountant, or use the figures to complete your Schedule C if you file independently.

**Q: How often does the AI forecast update?**
The cash flow forecast updates once per day and immediately after you add new transactions. If you just entered a large batch of transactions, click the **Refresh** icon on the forecast widget to get an updated projection immediately.

**Q: Can my accountant access Ledgr?**
Yes. Invite them as a **Viewer** or **Editor** from **Settings > Team** (Professional plan required — $59.99/mo). Viewer access lets them see all your data and run reports without being able to change anything.

**Q: What is the difference between the Starter and Professional plans?**
The Starter plan ($29.99/mo) includes all core features, all AI features, bank feed connections, up to 20 invoices per month, and 1 user. The Professional plan ($59.99/mo) removes the invoice limit, adds multi-currency support, time tracking, accountant access, and allows up to 3 users. Both plans include the AI chat assistant, receipt scanner, and all 7 financial reports. All new users receive a 14-day free trial with full Professional access — no credit card required.
